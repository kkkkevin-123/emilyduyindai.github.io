Uncovering Immune Evasion Mechanisms in Breast Cancer Cells

Abstract

In this project, I investigated how breast cancer cells evade immune system surveillance. I focused on key protein–protein interactions within the tumor microenvironment that influence immune signaling and cellular communication. By examining how cancer-related pathways alter immune recognition, I aimed to provide insights into potential therapeutic strategies that could help restore immune activity against tumor cells.

Contribution

I performed Western blotting and co-immunoprecipitation experiments to study protein interactions involved in immune evasion. I troubleshot and optimized the protocols to improve reproducibility and detection accuracy.
