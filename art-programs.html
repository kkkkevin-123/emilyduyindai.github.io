JHU Summer Pre-College: Anatomy, Physiology & Disease

In the summer of 2023, I attended the Johns Hopkins University Summer Pre-College Program in Anatomy, Physiology & Disease, taught by Dr. Ciarleglio. The course deepened my understanding of human biology and introduced me to the intricate connections between body systems and disease mechanisms. During lab sessions, I performed dissections on a cow’s eye, sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, tracing the optic nerve and examining real organs for the first time — an experience that made the science I’d learned in textbooks come vividly to life. We also explored neuroscience and cellular communication, which strengthened my interest in how biology translates into medicine and human health.
