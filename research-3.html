Sustainable Bioconversion of Paper Waste Using Synthetic Biology (iGEM HS-Alpha)

Abstract

In this project, I explored how synthetic biology can convert paper waste into valuable biomaterials, aligning our work with the UN Sustainable Development Goals on Responsible Consumption and Sustainable Cities. We designed experiments to engineer microbial systems capable of producing functional biomaterials from cellulose, aiming to create sustainable and scalable solutions for waste management.

Contribution

As Wet Lab Leader for iGEM HS-Alpha, I performed key molecular biology techniques, including agarose gel electrophoresis, gel extraction, and protein electrophoresis. I also organized and analyzed experimental data to optimize our protocols. Beyond the bench, I conducted interviews with synthetic biology experts to inform our project design and ensure practical applicability. Leading both the lab and the research strategy strengthened my skills in experimental planning, teamwork, and scientific communication.
